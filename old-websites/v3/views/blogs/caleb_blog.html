(Another) mystery solved by Joshua the Genius!

Wow, I have quite an imagination.

The backstory:

A previous mystery solved a while ago was how Caleb used to always go downstairs and "play." Well, I wasn't stupid! I knew how he will always log off the computer when I went downstairs, and, out of curiosity, logged into his account and figured out that he was watching an excessive amount of minecraft videos.

Snooping? No! He proudly admitted a while before that he had 'no secrets to hide' from anyone. That was why he didn't care that everyone in the family knew his password. It might feel wrong, I guess, to go onto his computer, but in a rational point of view, looking at his computer history was completely okay.

I kept quiet. No need to be a douchebag and tell mom directly. Who needs to when you can test a person's brain functions and have fun? So I subtly (maybe not really) tested him around. Testing questions were like "Hey, what did you do downstairs?" Maybe not the subtlest, but I integraded those questions with slight accusations like: "Caleb, why are you doing all of your project this Sunday night? You had so much time to do it on Saturday! What were you doing?" Since I knew that he was spending all of his time on Youtube, it was more of a mocking question than a real one, though I doubt he knew that.

I tried to do it when mom or someone was around so he can get more nervous and I can see what his reactions were. He will also be more forced to answer since he is the mommy's boy, not try to escape it. Mom would say, "yes, son, what were you doing?" and he will have to answer, lie, or escape the question with even more cunningness. He is actually not bad in lying, though. He will just makes himself look embarassed, look to the ground, and say something like: "Eh, I was playing." Since people will regard him as the "cute boy of the family," he can easily got away with things like this. Plus, he practically had the entire basement to himself, so the "playing" lie was not to badly put togather since mom would think that he was playing with the toys in the basement.

No need to get angry. I was learning new things. I decided to try to 'accidentally' figure out that he was on Youtube. It worked once, where I saw him suspend the computer (linux term for sleep) with the minecraft video on it. Then I teased him a bitand made him really uncomfortable. I was trying to help him get off that "addiction," if you would, but it just kept going on, and he just became smarter. Smarter, but not smart enough.

I knew that he is conscious of this because he will always try to hide it. In a rational point of view, there is nothing wrong with watching Youtube videos. Caleb has a little work load, and if he has free time, he has all the right to watch people uselessly do gaming videos to waste his time. I watch Youtube, too. But when it gets into the way of homework and Kumon, it starts to become bad. Mom dissapproves of minecraft game watching, so, in that case, I can see why Caleb is scared.

I remember when I used to watch Minecraft videos in the grade 4, 5 and 6 era. Maybe in the 5's the most. They were rather interesting, but it wore off and now I don't anymore! I guess that is sort of a big accomplishment.

The Present

Now he has found the shiny, high tech new invention called incognito mode. Whoa. He can browse with the searches not appearing on your search history! Well, that might of made his neocortex make the act of watching minecraft videos more morally pleasing, but it doesn't really change a thing. Oh yeah, it might of also decreased his stress since now people like me won't look at his search history and find minecraft videos being watched for 4 hours straight (I am pretty sure Caleb knows that I know that he watches minecraft videos extraneously). Now they will look and find nothing, even though he was downstairs for four hours! Of course evryone will think that he was playing with lego and tinkertoys for four hours each weekday! That new method is so inconspicuous, right guys? Of course that is!
But to be honest, I think it is mainly the first reason since he might not have the every-present suspicion that someone will browse through his search history.

He also got a lot more careful in running away to "play" before someone sees him watch minecraft videos. Before I heard dad a few times see Caleb watch the gaming vids, and would say "Oohh, always playing!" and probably annoy him. Since it wasn't mom, Caleb didn't take it really seriously, but he still took more precautions nevertheless.

Now he suspending the computer far before anyone can walk down the stairs to see him. I remember the funny times when he was still learning, and I would see him jump – literally jump! – out of his seat to the place where all the toys were. They were around 2 meters away, but he had to turn his swivel chair around to be able to run there. It was funny to watch, since he was in such a paniced mode. Lol.

Every since mom started working, coming home at 5 to 6 pm, he started to make sure he was always ready to cover up his video watching when he heard the garage door open – signalling that she was coming home. He would run upstairs and either say: "Hi mom!" or hide behind the basement door or corner after the garage door leading into the house to scare her. "Scaring" people was not new. He started...I actually don't know now. Two, three years ago? But all I know is that his boo's got really lame after a month of usage.

He can hide the fact that he watches minecraft videos from mom, but he can't hide the fact that he sacrifices kumon time for Youtube. See, every week, on Thursday, we would go to the kumon center, exchange our six homework booklets, do some work there, and get six new kumon booklets from them. It was designed for us to do one booklet a day. Not bad. Well, there were times when Caleb will not do any Kumon until Tuesday. Good job, Caleb!
But this is serious. Mom even talked to him about finishing his kumon on time, and he still doesn't do it. Seriously, this is DEFCON 1! Caleb, not following what mom says? Men, prepare for imminent nuclear war. Just kidding.

The Case

Over the winter break, there were no kumon classes for two weeks. We got 20 worksheets then to do one every day over the break. I knew that Caleb was lagging behind tremendously on the tenth day or so, and lagging behind as in not having done a single booklet, so I decided to go and regularly check how his kumon was doing. On the 13th day, mom found out, and made him do two every day. I would have liked him to cram it until the last day, since it would have been funny, but he didn't. He did enough everyday and got it finished. Or did he? The math obviusly did not work out, and I also checked his booklet on Wednesday, and he wasn't finished. I decided to wait until he went to the kumon center to get caught not finishing his kumon, but he came out unscathed. I was baffled. What happened there? I have never come to a kumon place with unfinished booklets, except for one time when I had questions about factorizing in about J50. That was excusable. Yes I was the good boy.
I checked his booklet when he came back and there were the rest of his booklets. Okay, I know where they went.

A few days later, though, those extra booklets were gone. He finished them? Impossible. I just knew that he didn't do that. So where did he put it? The recycle? Garbage? In his desk drawers? Nope. I was confused, but not frustrated. Maybe he put it in the recycle every Wednesday morning, when there is garbage day. He might have ingeniously put it somewhere, but I would be bound to find it.

Found

Under his chair cushion. A pretty stupid place to put it, if you ask me. I would have thought that he found a better place to hide/dispose his unuseful, not finished booklets, but nope. That is what happens when you procrastinate, I guess.

On his chair, there is a small cusion that connects to the supports behind it. It is designed to make the chair more comfortable, which does work. It also serves as a sketchy place to hide your kumon booklets!
